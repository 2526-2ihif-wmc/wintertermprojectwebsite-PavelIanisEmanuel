Samsung


Samsung, based in Korea, is the biggest smartphone manufacturer in the world. Samsung in its history made airplanes, tanks and cars. Nowadays, their products range from phones and consumer tech to medical systems, buildings like the Burj Khalifa and chips.

Phones have a "Galaxy" prefix which points to the many possibilities there are and how good the ecosystem integration is. Samsung is known for its high quality and premium prices.

Samsung has something called Galaxy AI, a full set of the most advanced AI features just like the Pixel phones. Whether you want to edit some photos by erasing objects or adding some, or you want to live translate a conversation, Galaxy AI makes it possible.

A downside of all Samsung phones are the slow charging speeds (45W max).

A Series - Mid-range & Budget


The A series is Samsung's main mid-range and budget series. The phones themselves don't have crazy features like other brands, but they offer reliable cheap phones with good features.

Their naming scheme seems complicated at first, but is really easy once you understand it. The number consists of 2 digits. The first one indicates the tier (0 is the cheapest). The second digit shows the generation. Right now, the newest generation is the x6. The 07 and 17 already got released, with the 27, 37 and 57 expected to come soon. There used to be tiers from 0 through 9, but nowadays less phones are released. A well-known A series phone is the 80, with its special pop-up camera. Suffixes at the end like e or s indicate a slightly cheaper version of the base model, but nowadays those aren't used anymore.
